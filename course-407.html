Course-407

Arabic Literary Criticism

Course Overview

This course provides a comprehensive exploration of Arabic literary criticism, tracing its evolution from the classical period to contemporary approaches. Students will engage with seminal critical texts and key critical figures, analyzing the theoretical frameworks that have shaped the understanding and evaluation of Arabic literature.

Intended Learning Outcomes

On completion of the course students will be able to:

  • Identify major schools and trends in the history of Arabic literary criticism.
  • Analyze the critical methodologies of prominent Arab critics and their intellectual projects.
  • Apply various critical theories to the analysis of classical and modern Arabic literary texts.
  • Understand the influence of Western critical thought on modern Arabic criticism.
  • Critically evaluate the arguments in a piece of literary criticism.
  • Develop and articulate their own critical interpretations of literary works.
