Course-405

Modern Poetry II

Course Overview

The course familiarises the students with the renowned emigrant Arab or Mahjar poets and the poets of Diwan school. The course focuses on the emergence of Mahjar Literature and its significance.

Intended Learning Outcomes

On completion of the course students will be able to:

  • Learn about mahjar poetry for its adoption of modern literary styles.
  • Learn about the mahjar poets who literally have revolted against the literary orthodoxy and have adopted free verses in poetry.
  • Gain knowledge for making Arabic poetry more people friendly, fascinating and easily understandable.
  • Earn sufficient information about simple diction, style and stanzalc form with short metres and techniques of expression borrowed from drama and narratives provided in the hallmarks of poetry in the Arab and mahjar worlds.
  • Be familiar with the characteristics (forms, techniques and themes) of different genres in modern Arabic and western literature and trace their histories and interactions.
  • Know about the evolution of literary genres in the Arab and western worlds in their socio-cultural context, keeping in mind the dynamics of unity and diversity, and continuity and change.
